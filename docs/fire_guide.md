## What is FIRE?

FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early) is a movement focused on achieving financial independence through:
- High savings rates (50%+ of income)
- Smart investing (index funds, diversification)
- Living below your means
- Building passive income streams

**Goal:** Save enough to retire decades before traditional retirement age (65).

---

## The Core Principles

### 1. The 4% Rule

**Foundation of FIRE:** You need 25x your annual expenses saved.

```
FIRE Number = Annual Expenses √ó 25
```

**Why 25?** Based on the Trinity Study:
- Withdraw 4% annually from your portfolio
- Historically safe for 30+ year retirement
- Accounts for market volatility

**Example:**
```python
Annual Expenses: $40,000
FIRE Number: $40,000 √ó 25 = $1,000,000

Can safely withdraw: $40,000/year (4%)
```

---

### 2. Savings Rate is King

**Most important factor:** NOT your salary, but % you save.

**Years to FIRE by Savings Rate:**

| Savings Rate | Years to FIRE |
|--------------|---------------|
| 10%          | 51 years      |
| 25%          | 32 years      |
| 50%          | 17 years      |
| 70%          | 9 years       |

**Formula:**
```
Years = log(1 + savings_rate / withdrawal_rate) / log(1 + return_rate)
```

---

### 3. Compound Interest Magic

**Einstein:** "Compound interest is the 8th wonder of the world"

**Rule of 72:**
```
Years to double = 72 / annual return rate

8% return ‚Üí doubles every 9 years
10% return ‚Üí doubles every 7.2 years
```

**Example:**
```
$10,000 invested at 8% annual return:
Year 0: $10,000
Year 9: $20,000
Year 18: $40,000
Year 27: $80,000
Year 36: $160,000
```

## FIRE Variations

### 1. Lean FIRE
- Minimal expenses ($20k-30k/year)
- Frugal lifestyle
- Fastest to achieve
- Example: $500k-750k needed

### 2. Regular FIRE
- Comfortable lifestyle ($40k-50k/year)
- Moderate spending
- Balanced approach
- Example: $1M-1.25M needed

### 3. Fat FIRE
- Luxurious lifestyle ($100k+/year)
- No sacrifice
- Takes longer
- Example: $2.5M+ needed

### 4. Coast FIRE
- Save enough early
- Let it grow to FIRE
- Can work part-time
- Example: $200k at 30 ‚Üí $1M at 50

### 5. Barista FIRE
- Cover basics with part-time work
- Portfolio covers extras
- Lower FIRE number needed
- Example: $500k + $20k/year job

## Step-by-Step FIRE Plan

### Phase 1: Calculate Your Numbers

1. **Track Expenses (3 months minimum)**
   ```
   Fixed: Rent, utilities, insurance
   Variable: Food, transport, entertainment
   Annual total: _______
   ```

2. **Calculate FIRE Number**
   ```
   Annual Expenses √ó 25 = FIRE Number
   ```

3. **Current Net Worth**
   ```
   Assets - Liabilities = Net Worth
   ```

4. **Gap to FIRE**
   ```
   FIRE Number - Net Worth = Gap
   ```

### Phase 2: Increase Savings Rate

**Income Side:**
- Negotiate raise
- Switch jobs
- Side hustle
- Freelance work

**Expense Side:**
- Track every dollar
- Cut recurring subscriptions
- House hack (roommates)
- Optimize big 3: Housing, transport, food

**Target:** 50%+ savings rate

### Phase 3: Invest Wisely

**Asset Allocation:**
```
Aggressive (20s-30s):
- 90% stocks
- 10% bonds

Moderate (40s):
- 70% stocks
- 30% bonds

Conservative (50s+):
- 50% stocks
- 50% bonds
```

**Investment Vehicles:**
- Index funds (VOO, VTI)
- Target date funds
- Real estate
- Bonds for stability

**Avoid:**
- Individual stock picking
- Crypto as core holding
- High-fee funds
- Market timing

### Phase 4: Monitor & Adjust

**Monthly:**
- Track net worth
- Review spending
- Rebalance if needed

**Yearly:**
- Calculate progress %
- Adjust savings rate
- Tax optimization
- Review FIRE number

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Too Conservative
- Keeping cash (inflation kills)
- Not investing early
- **Fix:** Start investing NOW

### 2. Too Aggressive
- 100% stocks at 55
- YOLO into crypto
- **Fix:** Age-appropriate allocation

### 3. Lifestyle Inflation
- Salary increases ‚Üí spending increases
- **Fix:** Save raises, not spend

### 4. Not Tracking
- "Rough estimate" of spending
- Surprised by expenses
- **Fix:** Track every dollar for 3 months

### 5. Ignoring Healthcare
- US: Biggest FIRE expense
- Need coverage until Medicare
- **Fix:** Budget $500-1000/month
